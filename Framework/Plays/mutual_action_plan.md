# Mutual Action Plan (MAP) Play: Joint Buyer-Seller Success Planning

<triggers>
- complex_buying_process (multiple approval stages, long cycle)
- long_sales_cycle (3+ months from qualify to close)
- multiple_approvals (committee, board, procurement, legal, security)
- timeline_slippage_risk (deal dragging, no clear next steps)
- champion_needs_help (champion wants to drive internally but needs structure)
- executive_alignment (exec sponsor wants visibility into progress)
</triggers>

<principles>
- **Joint ownership**: Both parties commit to timeline and milestones (not just vendor pushing)
- **Specific dates**: "CFO review by March 15" (not "CFO review in a few weeks")
- **Named owners**: Every milestone has customer + vendor owner (not vague "team will handle")
- **Weekly reviews**: Check-in cadence to track progress and address blockers
- **Executive sponsors**: Both sides have exec sponsor (ensures accountability)
- **Living document**: Update weekly, not "set and forget"
</principles>

<steps>
1. **Identify need for MAP**:
   - **Good fit**: Complex deals (3+ stakeholders), long cycles (60+ days), multiple approvals
   - **Poor fit**: Simple deals (single buyer, 30-day cycle), transactional purchases
   - **Timing**: Introduce in Stage 2 (Qualify) or Stage 3 (Propose), not Stage 1 (too early)

2. **Co-create with champion**:
   - **Workshop session** (30-45 min with champion):
     - "Let's map out the path to get this approved"
     - Identify all approval gates (technical, business, legal, procurement, exec)
     - Assign dates and owners
   - **Champion involvement is critical**: They own customer-side milestones (not AE dictating)

3. **Structure the MAP** (use template):
   - **Milestone**: What needs to happen
   - **Owner**: Customer lead + Vendor lead
   - **Due Date**: Specific date (not "Week of" or "Mid-month")
   - **Status**: Not Started / In Progress / Completed / Blocked
   - **Notes**: Any blockers or dependencies

4. **Get executive sponsor buy-in**:
   - Present MAP to customer exec sponsor (CFO, CIO, etc.) for approval
   - Exec sponsor commits to timeline and resources
   - AE's exec sponsor (VP Sales, CRO) also commits

5. **Weekly check-ins**:
   - **15-min sync** every Monday (or Friday) with champion
   - Review: What's done? What's blocked? What's next?
   - Update MAP status in real-time
   - Escalate blockers to exec sponsors if needed

6. **Monitor and adapt**:
   - **Green**: All milestones on track
   - **Yellow**: 1-2 milestones slipping, but recoverable
   - **Red**: 3+ milestones delayed or exec sponsor disengaged (deal at risk)
   - **Adapt**: If timeline slips, adjust MAP and re-commit (don't let it go stale)
</steps>

<example id="executive_sponsor_disengagement">
**Context**: $1.8M deal with MAP, exec sponsor (CIO) initially engaged, then went silent

**Situation**: MAP shows 3 milestones delayed, champion can't get CIO's time

**Red flag**: Exec sponsor disengagement = deal at risk

**Action**: Escalated to vendor exec sponsor (VP Sales)

**Escalation**:
- VP Sales reached out to CIO (peer-to-peer): "I see we've fallen behind on timeline. Is this still a priority?"
- CIO response: "Yes, but we had internal re-org. Let me re-engage."
- CIO assigned new owner (Dir IT) and re-committed to MAP timeline

**Outcome**: Deal recovered, closed 3 weeks later (exec re-engagement saved the deal)

**Key moves**:
- MAP visibility flagged exec disengagement early (not months later)
- Vendor exec sponsor escalated (peer-to-peer CIO outreach)
- Re-committed to MAP with new owner (Dir IT)
</example>

**Drafted MAP**:

|-----------|---------------------------|----------|--------|

**Champion reaction**: "This is super helpful, I'll show this to my CIO"

<example id="map_rejection">
**Context**: $700K deal, proposed MAP, champion rejected it

**Situation**: Champion says "We don't work that way, we'll just handle it internally"

**Red flag**: Rejection of MAP = lack of commitment or hiding something

**Action**: Tested champion commitment

**AE response**: "I understand. Can you at least share the internal approval process and timeline?"

**Champion**: "I don't have that information yet"

**Follow-up**: "When do you think you'll have a timeline? I want to make sure we support you."

**Champion**: "I'm not sure, we're still figuring it out"

**Diagnosis**: Champion is not driving internally (weak champion) or deal is not prioritized

**Outcome**: Deal stalled for 6 weeks, eventually lost to competitor who had stronger champion

**Key moves**:
- MAP rejection was early warning signal (champion not committed)
- Tested champion with follow-up questions (confirmed weak champion)
- Should have pivoted to find stronger champion or disqualified earlier
</example>
</examples>

<pitfalls>
- **Vendor-only ownership**: MAP with only vendor driving = not mutual, customer won't commit
- **Vague milestones**: "Legal review sometime in March" (not specific) = no accountability
- **Set and forget**: Creating MAP but never updating = wasted effort, stale document
- **Too complex**: 30+ milestones = overwhelming, nobody will use it (keep to 6-10 major milestones)
- **No exec sponsor**: MAP without exec buy-in = champion can't enforce timeline
- **Introducing too early**: MAP in Stage 1 (Discovery) = premature, buyer isn't committed yet
</pitfalls>
