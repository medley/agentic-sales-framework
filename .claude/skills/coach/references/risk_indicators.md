## Economic Buyer Disengagement

**Indicators:**
- Economic Buyer delegated evaluation entirely to team
- Economic Buyer canceled or no-showed executive alignment call
- Economic Buyer has not confirmed budget availability directly
- Economic Buyer unaware of deal progress (discovered via champion)
- Economic Buyer focused on different priorities (per recent company news)
- Economic Buyer not present in final decision discussions

**Severity Assessment:**
- ðŸ”´ **Critical:** Economic Buyer never identified or engaged
- ðŸŸ¡ **High:** Economic Buyer engaged once, now disengaged
- ðŸŸ  **Medium:** Economic Buyer engaged but delegating heavily

**Mitigation Actions:**
- Schedule executive briefing (15-min max, business outcomes only)
- Leverage champion to create Economic Buyer urgency
- Align solution to Economic Buyer's personal/organizational OKRs
- Identify compelling event tied to Economic Buyer's goals

## Competition Displacement Risk

**Indicators:**
- Competitor mentioned favorably by stakeholders
- Evaluation criteria heavily favor competitor's strengths
- Stakeholders referencing competitor's terminology/positioning
- Competitor has existing relationship (incumbent or partner)
- Procurement comparing your pricing to competitor's quote
- Technical evaluation focused on feature parity with competitor

**Severity Assessment:**
- ðŸ”´ **Critical:** Incumbent competitor with multi-year contract renewal approaching
- ðŸŸ¡ **High:** Competitor actively engaged, stakeholder preference unclear
- ðŸŸ  **Medium:** Competitor mentioned, no active engagement detected

**Mitigation Actions:**
- Load battlecard for competitor, identify differentiation
- Shift evaluation criteria to your strengths (via champion influence)
- Create urgency around competitor's weaknesses (risk, cost, timeline)
- Position as complementary vs. competitive if displacement unlikely

## Timeline Compression Risk

**Indicators:**
- Close date moved up significantly (e.g., Q4 â†’ Q2)
- Stakeholder requesting proposal/pricing before discovery complete
- Customer bypassing standard procurement/legal review
- Executive pressure to "move faster" without clear compelling event
- Stakeholder unwilling to schedule adequate evaluation time
- Proposal deadline before technical validation complete

**Severity Assessment:**
- ðŸ”´ **Critical:** Artificial urgency with no business compelling event (budget burn, competitor threat)
- ðŸŸ¡ **High:** Legitimate urgency but skipping qualification steps
- ðŸŸ  **Medium:** Timeline aggressive but stakeholders committed

**Mitigation Actions:**
- Qualify compelling event (why now vs. next quarter?)
- Sandler: "I'm concerned we'll move too fast and miss critical requirements. What's driving the urgency?"
- If no compelling event: Slow down, risk of losing to "no decision"
- If legitimate event: Accelerate, but don't skip qualification (compress timelines in parallel)

## Budget Authority Gap

**Indicators:**
- Budget "should be available" but not confirmed by Economic Buyer
- Stakeholder deflecting budget questions ("we'll find it if it makes sense")
- Budget allocated to different initiative, requires reallocation
- Multi-year deal but budget only confirmed for Year 1
- Budget tied to executive approval that hasn't occurred
- Procurement negotiating price before budget confirmed

**Severity Assessment:**
- ðŸ”´ **Critical:** No budget discussion occurred, Economic Buyer not engaged on cost
- ðŸŸ¡ **High:** Budget exists but authority unclear or requires executive approval
- ðŸŸ  **Medium:** Budget confirmed by champion, awaiting Economic Buyer validation

**Mitigation Actions:**
- Sandler: "Have you thought about budget?" â†’ If no, disqualify or move to budget conversation
- Direct question to Economic Buyer: "Is ${X} within your allocated budget for this initiative?"
- Identify budget approval process: who signs, what threshold requires C-suite approval?
- Confirm budget timing: available now, next quarter, fiscal year change?

## Decision Process Opacity

**Indicators:**
- Stakeholder can't describe decision-making process
- "We'll know it when we see it" (no criteria defined)
- Multiple stakeholders with veto power, no clear DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)
- Evaluation committee membership unclear
- Decision timeline vague ("probably Q3 sometime")
- Procurement involved but authority vs. influencer role unclear

**Severity Assessment:**
- ðŸ”´ **Critical:** No defined decision process, timeline, or criteria
- ðŸŸ¡ **High:** Process described but stakeholders disagree on details
- ðŸŸ  **Medium:** Process described, awaiting final validation from Economic Buyer

**Mitigation Actions:**
- MEDDPICC: Map paper process explicitly (who signs, in what order, by when?)
- Ask champion: "Walk me through the last time you purchased similar software. Who was involved? How long did it take?"
- Identify approval chain: Technical â†’ Economic Buyer â†’ Procurement â†’ Legal â†’ CFO?
- Get champion to drive internal alignment on decision criteria

## Deal Stagnation

**Indicators:**
- No customer interaction in 14+ days
- Deal in same stage for 30+ days (typical: 14-21 days per stage)
- Stakeholder saying "we're still evaluating" with no specific next steps
- Meetings canceled/rescheduled multiple times
- Champion not driving internal urgency
- No forward progress artifacts (no demo scheduled, no proposal requested)

**Severity Assessment:**
- ðŸ”´ **Critical:** 30+ days no contact, champion non-responsive
- ðŸŸ¡ **High:** 14-30 days slow progress, meetings rescheduled
- ðŸŸ  **Medium:** 7-14 days, stakeholder engaged but slow

**Mitigation Actions:**
- Re-qualify compelling event: "Has anything changed since we last spoke?"
- Sandler: "It seems like this might not be a priority right now. Should we revisit in Q3?"
- Offer to disqualify: If no urgency, free up your time and theirs
- Create urgency: Time-limited offer, competitive threat, risk of status quo

## Pain Intensity Insufficient

**Indicators (Sandler-specific, applicable to all methodologies):**
- Stakeholder using intellectual language: "We're thinking about...", "It would be nice to..."
- No emotional language: Frustration, fear, risk, urgency absent
- Problem existed for months/years without action (no catalyst)
- Pain rated below 7/10 when asked directly
- Stakeholder can't quantify cost of status quo (time, money, risk)
- Solution positioned as "nice to have" vs "must have"

**Severity Assessment:**
- ðŸ”´ **Critical:** Pain below 5/10, no compelling event, intellectual framing only
- ðŸŸ¡ **High:** Pain 5-7/10, some urgency but not personal to Economic Buyer
- ðŸŸ  **Medium:** Pain 7+/10 for champion, awaiting Economic Buyer validation

**Mitigation Actions:**
- Sandler Pain Funnel: "How long has this been a problem? What have you tried? What's it costing you?"
- Escalate to personal impact: "If this doesn't get solved, what happens to your team/goals/role?"
- Quantify cost: "What's the financial impact of continuing as-is for another 6 months?"
- If pain insufficient: Disqualify or nurture until pain increases (external event, new mandate)
